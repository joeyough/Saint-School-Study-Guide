Bible Study · Discussion Guide

Philippians 3

A guided discussion on counting all things as loss, the pursuit of knowing Christ, and pressing toward the upward call.

18 Discussion Questions
Page One
Warning, Credentials, Knowing Christ
Q 01 · Verse 1
Paul says to rejoice in the Lord. He's writing from prison. How is that possible? What's the difference between joy and happiness?
Q 02 · Verses 2–3
Paul calls them dogs, evil workers, and mutilators of the flesh. Who is he talking about, and why such aggressive language?
Judaizers — those requiring circumcision for salvation
Galatians 5:12 — Paul uses even stronger language
"We are the circumcision, who worship by the Spirit of God"
Q 03 · Verses 4–6
Paul lists his résumé: circumcised, tribe of Benjamin, Pharisee, zealous, blameless under the law. What would the modern church equivalent of this list look like?
Raised in church, seminary degree, mission trips, worship team
Social media following, leadership positions, tithes
Q 04 · Verses 7–8
He counted all of that as loss — then goes further and calls it rubbish. What's the difference between those two words? Why the escalation?
Greek: skubalon — dung, refuse, table scraps thrown to dogs
Not just worthless — an obstacle to the real thing
Q 05 · Verse 8
What is the difference between knowing about Christ and knowing Christ? How do you move from one to the other?
Q 06 · Verse 9
What is the difference between a righteousness of my own that comes from the law and that which comes through faith in Christ?
Self-righteousness vs. imputed righteousness
Romans 10:3 — "Seeking to establish their own"
Isaiah 64:6 — "All our righteous deeds are like filthy rags"
Q 07 · Verses 10–11
Paul wants to know the power of his resurrection and the fellowship of his sufferings. Why does he pair those two together? Can you have one without the other?
Romans 6:3–5 — United with him in death and resurrection
2 Corinthians 4:10 — Carrying the death of Jesus in our body
1 Peter 4:13 — Rejoice in sharing Christ's sufferings
Q 08 · Personal Reflection
What are you still putting confidence in that isn't Christ?
Q 09 · Verse 11
Paul says that by any means possible he may attain the resurrection. Does that phrase surprise you? What does it reveal about his posture?
Not doubt in salvation — desperation in pursuit
Humility: even Paul doesn't presume on God's grace

Page Two
Pressing On, Maturity, Citizenship
Q 10 · Verse 12
Paul says not that I have already obtained this or am already perfect. How does this challenge the idea of "arrived" Christianity?
Q 11 · Verses 13–14
Forgetting what lies behind and straining forward to what lies ahead. What do you need to forget? What does it look like to strain forward?
"One thing I do" — not ten things, not five. One.
Past failures and past achievements can both hold you back
Q 12 · Verse 14
What is the prize of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus?
2 Timothy 4:7–8 — "The crown of righteousness"
Not just heaven — the fullness of knowing Christ forever
Q 13 · Verse 15
Let those of us who are mature think this way. What does spiritual maturity actually look like? How is it different from what the world calls maturity?
Q 14 · Verse 16
Let us hold true to what we have attained. What has God already revealed to you that you're not yet walking in?
Q 15 · Verse 17
Paul says join in imitating me. Is it appropriate for a leader to say "follow my example"? When does that become dangerous?
1 Corinthians 11:1 — "Imitate me, as I imitate Christ"
The qualifier matters: only as far as they follow Christ
Q 16 · Verses 18–19
Who are the enemies of the cross? What does it mean that their god is their stomach and they glory in their shame?
Romans 16:18 — "They serve their own appetites"
"Their minds are set on earthly things" — the root issue
Q 17 · Verse 20
Our citizenship is in heaven. How should this identity shape the way we engage with politics, culture, and daily life?
Q 18 · Verse 21
Christ will transform our lowly body to be like his glorious body. How does the promise of future transformation affect how you live right now?
1 John 3:2 — "When he appears, we shall be like him"
Romans 8:18 — Present sufferings not worth comparing
Closing Thought
May this study ignite a holy dissatisfaction with everything that isn't Christ, and a relentless pursuit of the One who pursued us first.
